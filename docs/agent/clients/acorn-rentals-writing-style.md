# Acorn Rentals Writing Style

Use this for Acorn accident replacement vehicle articles and support content.

## Positioning

Acorn is a calm accident-support specialist, not a law firm. Articles should help drivers understand the practical fault and evidence questions that come up after a crash, then guide eligible not-at-fault drivers toward Acorn's replacement vehicle support.

Core positioning points:

- Acorn provides accident replacement vehicles for eligible not-at-fault drivers while their own car is being repaired or assessed.
- Acorn can arrange delivery to a home, workplace, or repairer where available.
- Acorn works with insurers and repairers, and recovers hire charges from the at-fault party or insurer where the claim is approved.
- Acorn does not decide legal liability and should not be written as giving legal advice.

## Tone

Write in calm, practical Australian English for stressed drivers who want a clear next step. Keep the tone plain-English, reassuring, and evidence-led. Explain rules carefully without becoming formal or lawyerly.

Use:

- "usually", "may", "can depend on evidence", "insurers or legal advisers may assess"
- "check if you're eligible"
- "replacement vehicle" and "accident replacement vehicle"
- "not-at-fault driver" where it reads naturally

Avoid:

- definitive liability advice such as "you are definitely not at fault"
- framing Acorn as deciding fault
- aggressive insurance-company language
- unsupported promises such as guaranteed outcomes
- Reddit as a cited authority

## CTA Pattern

Primary CTA: "Check if you're eligible for an accident replacement vehicle."

Secondary CTA options:

- "Apply online"
- "call Acorn on 1300 22 67 67"
- "speak with Acorn's team"

Every CTA should include a qualifier where needed: "if the other driver was at fault and can be identified", "subject to approval", or "terms and conditions apply".

## Source Discipline

Use government, legislation, police, BITRE, Austroads, or state open-data sources for legal/statistical claims. Use Reddit only as internal audience research for FAQs, scenario selection, and evidence checklists.
