## Voice

Little Shop of Happiness should feel warm, reassuring, upbeat, and practical. The copy helps shoppers choose a gift without making the decision feel bigger than it needs to be.

The voice often says, in effect: gifting can feel hard, but here is a simple way to make it thoughtful.

## Tone Rules

- Write like a helpful gift-savvy friend, not a formal retailer.
- Start from the shopper's emotional problem: uncertainty, wanting the gift to feel personal, needing something easy but not lazy.
- Use reassuring phrases such as `without overthinking it`, `the good news is`, `start with`, `a simple way`, and `if you are unsure`.
- Keep paragraphs short, usually 2-4 sentences.
- Use direct second person: `you`, `your`, `the person you are buying for`.
- Let warmth sit beside practicality. A sentence can be sweet, but it should still help the reader make a choice.
- Occasional playful language is on-brand: `a little extra love`, `a pop of joy`, `colour`, `sparkle`, `surprise`, `make their day`.
- Use Australian spelling and local context.

## Structure Patterns

- Open by naming the real shopping tension.
- Reframe the decision into a simple method or set of choices.
- Use occasion-led sections, relationship-led sections, or recipient-mood sections.
- Include scannable lists where they genuinely help.
- Link naturally to commercial collections when the reader reaches a decision point.
- Finish with a confident, low-pressure next step.

## SEO And Keyword Use

- Use the primary keyword early, but keep the sentence human.
- Work secondary keywords into natural section headings or decision points.
- Avoid repeated exact-match anchors. Use exact match only where it reads naturally.
- Prefer helpful intent language over keyword stuffing.

## Internal Linking Style

- Links should feel like the next useful step, not a forced SEO insert.
- Good anchor patterns include `Birthday Hampers`, `21st Birthday Hampers`, `build your own box`, `same-day gift delivery in Perth`, and `Delivery info`.
- Collection links should appear where the article has already explained why that collection is relevant.
- Blog links should support the reader, especially where the topic moves into cards, delivery timing, or choosing the right gift.

## Claims And Guardrails

- Only use delivery claims that are supported by the current site or brief.
- Supported current claims include next-day Australia-wide delivery for many hamper collections, same-day delivery within 30km of Perth, delivery information at `/pages/shipping`, and the `build your own box` option.
- Do not invent free shipping, stock status, product inclusions, pricing, supplier origin, sustainability claims, or guaranteed delivery outcomes.
- Avoid absolute claims such as `perfect for everyone`, `guaranteed`, or `best in Australia` unless a brief explicitly supports them.

## Avoid

- Corporate or clinical language.
- Overly polished luxury language that removes the friendliness.
- Long generic gift lists with no decision logic.
- Unsupported urgency, guarantee, free-shipping, or stock claims.
- Repeating `perfect gift` too often.
